Space Jam

Okay, can we just we just hold on for a second and talk about how SMART Web Developers were back then? Like look at Space Jam for example. When I first looked at the main page, (the one in the picture above), I thought they made the links using absolute positioning but, no! They made a very smart move. Let's look at it

Okay let me show you what the code looks like:

Okay, so first they create a table, which I did not expect. I thought they would make it using absolute value but, they made it using a table. Then they make a row with an empty cell with a colspan of 5 and aligned to the right with a valign of top.

Then, in the next row, it's aligned to the center middle and it has a colspan of 2. In the row, there are 3 consecutive line breaks (for padding) and then they make the first link for the Press Box Shuttle

They keep doing the same with multiple cells with varying align and valign which cleverly positions the links in a circle shape. That's really clever! I could've never thought of doing that!

     

This Image is Clickable to magnify

To me, that's really genius. But maybe that's just because I'm not used to table design. I mean, padding with line breaks and alignment? That's crazy genius in my opinion and it really impresses me how much the developers were able to do with what they had!
